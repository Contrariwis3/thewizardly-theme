A Half-Built Future

● Inscribed on January 25, 2026

The first paragraph of an essay is doing a particular kind of work. It has to invite the reader into a room and tell them, with no metadata, what kind of room it is. So this one commits: long-form writing on a personal site has a different shape than a one-line aside, and the page that holds both should honor the difference.

What follows is a floor you walk, not a feed you scroll: each exhibit is a different object, lit on its own terms, and the path runs from one to the next.
