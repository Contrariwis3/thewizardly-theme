A Half-Built Future

● Inscribed on January 25, 2026

The first paragraph of an essay is doing a particular kind of work. It has to invite the reader into a room and tell them, with no metadata, what kind of room it is. So this one commits: long-form writing on a personal site has a different shape than a one-line aside, and the page that holds both should honor the difference rather than flatten it.

What follows is a catalog, not a feed. A wall of essay text pulls all the oxygen out of a room that is supposed to hold a photograph, an aside, a status from the wire, and a recommendation, each doing real work in its own register. The front page renders the newest piece in full, then trims it at a paragraph boundary and lets the rest of the catalog breathe around it.

The drafting lamp falls on the sheet currently being written; the standing exhibits of the pavilion, the reading shelf, the dispatches on the wire, the allied mages, sit in the cooler light just past the pool, present but not competing.
