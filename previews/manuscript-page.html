The Wizardly

Of Cabbages and Wax Seals

▸ Inscribed on January 14, 2026

A short letter from the workbench. The candle is sage. The seal is rose. The room behind is inkwell-dark. Things are out, in use, mid-thought; that is the point of a workbench, and that is the point of a personal site. The illuminated capital is the medieval scribe's way of saying begin here, and it does the same job nine hundred years later: a clear, generous opening that earns the rest of the page.

Subsequent paragraphs need no flourish. The body face is Atkinson Hyperlegible Next, which carries every word a reader actually reads. Hyperlegibility is non-negotiable; atmosphere never wins over reading. The Philosopher serif is reserved for the heading, the drop cap, and small ornamental labels.

The voice belongs to the proprietor, not to the room. The room is just a person's workbench, faithfully rendered.

Notes from the margin

The dateline now lives in the left gutter on desktop, like a scribe's marginalia. It does not push the body text down; it floats in the negative space beside the page. On smaller screens it falls back inside the card, where it has always been.

A Second Page, For Comparison

▸ Inscribed on January 9, 2026

Each titled post opens with a fresh illumination. The drop cap is the system's this is an essay signal, replacing the side-stripe that used to sit on the left edge of every card. The card itself is now a clean translucent page; the shadow alone defines its edge against the inkwell body.

Successive cards stack with a 40-pixel gap. They never touch. The eye reads them as separate pages laid out on the workbench, each with its own dated note in the margin.

▸ Inscribed on January 7, 2026

A titleless micropost. No drop cap, no h2. Just a thought, a link, a small note. The card still gets its marginalia date; the body text reads plainly.
