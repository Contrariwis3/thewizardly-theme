# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Friends and concentric circles of people who already know Contrariwise the Wizardly. They arrive via direct link, RSS, micro.blog timeline, or by following a button or blogroll edge from another personal site. They're not strangers being converted into readers; they're people checking in.

The job to be done: keep up with what Contrariwise is reading, thinking, and doing, and find the connective tissue out to the rest of the indie web (status updates, blogroll, web buttons, bookshelf).

## Product Purpose

A personal blog at thewizardly.com, hosted on micro.blog and rendered by this Hugo theme. The site is a pavilion at an unending World's Fair: a place where Contrariwise writes posts, lists books, posts status updates from omg.lol, and trades buttons with the rest of the indie web — and where the act of doing those things is itself argued to be a small spell.

**Success criterion.** A friend who knows Contrariwise well opens the site, stars tearing up, and thinks: *god damnit, the bastard did it.* They would not have bet on the long shot landing; they are glad to be wrong; they can feel that the audacious commitment paid off. The site rewards being known by its author, and rewards believing in better futures, in the same gesture.

## Brand Personality

Playful, earnest, warm.

Play is fundamental here. Play is self-care. Play is our heartfelt and complete response to the horrors. We play. We dream. Whole-chest authenticity is the whole posture: hands in the clay, full-body commitment to the bit, no smirk, no remove. *Fuck it. We ball.*

The wizard here is a Dreamer. Wizardry, as practiced at this site, is the discipline of refusing to be cynical: writing essays, reading widely, posting status updates from omg.lol, trading 88×31 buttons, and pointing outward to other people's work are themselves arguments that a better world is still possible if we keep building toward it. The voice stays fully committed to the wizardly bit ("Inscribed on", "Mystic Missives", "Allied Mages", "Enchanted Emblems", "Teleportation Circle") — but the bit has matured. It is no longer curio-shop atmosphere; it *is* the spell.

We aspire to the mantle of the wizardry the world used to mean by the word — the lineage where the Imagineers were also wizards, where Sorcerer Mickey was a credible mascot for a serious adult craft of wonder, where the unbuilt EPCOT was a Real Plan, and where the Eameses, the 1964 World's Fair planners, and the Whole Earth Catalog were doing the same job from different drafting tables. These spirits are kin, not branding. The site stands beside them, never under them; they are never named in copy and never quoted. Their *posture* is inherited: sincere wonder, careful design, hopeful engineering.

The host is bookish and well-read but not gatekeepy: generous with attribution, generous with outbound links, happy when you wander off to a friend's site. Snark, edgelord posturing, ironic detachment, and self-importance are explicitly out. So is the mischief-as-prankster register — Loki, the Cheshire cat's smirk, the "isn't it funny that I care" remove. Those characters are cool; they just don't live here.

## Anti-references

This site should NOT look or feel like:

- **AI-rendered chrome-rocket atompunk.** Glossy chrome bezels, fake brushed-metal gradients, rendered-3D rocket fins, fluorescent vector starbursts. The Midjourney-atompunk slop pile. Real mid-century ephemera is paper, ink, screen-printed color, and laminated print — not gleaming CGI gloss.
- **Generic Jetsons / Saturday-morning cartoon retrofuture.** Saturated primaries on cartoon curves, exaggerated UFO shapes, ironic-mascot retrofuture. The wizard isn't a Hanna-Barbera character.
- **Direct Disney / EPCOT / Imagineer references.** The lineage is kin, not licensed. No Mickey, no Imagineer branding marks, no Tomorrowland callouts in copy, no Carousel-of-Progress glyphs. We stand beside Walt; we never invoke him by name.
- **Mad Men corporate mid-century.** Don Draper office cosplay — olive green, Helvetica, ad-agency restraint. Atompunk here is a person's pavilion, not an expense account.
- **Crypto-bro / SV techno-optimism.** "We're so back" hype, beanie-vector founders, neon-on-black manifesto type, NFT-rocket emoji. The optimism here is sincere, slow-burning, and built — not pumped.
- **Modern SaaS / Substack templates.** White sans-serif blog with author bio card, subscribe CTA, Medium-style scroll-optimized layout. This site is a hearth, not a funnel.
- **Goth / metal mysticism.** Pure black + blood red, occult sigils, blackletter, edgelord atmosphere. The base is night-shift midnight, not pitch black; the wizardry here is Dreaming, not occult.
- **Ironic Web 2.0 nostalgia bait.** Comic Sans, animated GIF chaos as meme costume, "old web" worn ironically. The 88×31 web buttons here are sincere indie-web participation, not pastiche.

## Design Principles

1. **Play and dream are the discipline.** Play is our heartfelt and complete response to the horrors. The site argues, sincerely and without irony, that a better world is still possible if we keep believing and building toward it. We play. We dream. The wizardly voice is the spell of refusing to be cynical *and* the freedom to mess about with full chest. If a flourish only works as a wink, it goes; if a flourish requires us to pretend not to care, it doubly goes.

2. **Stand beside the lineage, never under it.** The unbuilt EPCOT, the Imagineers, the Eames studio, the 1964 World's Fair, the Whole Earth Catalog — these are kin. Inherit their posture (sincere wonder, careful design, hopeful engineering) without naming or quoting them. The wizard aspires to the mantle of forgotten wizardries; the wizard does not wear someone else's costume.

3. **The wizard works the night shift.** Dark theme is not gothic atmosphere; it is the all-night drafting table, the planetarium dome, the model city humming under starlight. The wizard is awake while the world sleeps, holding the candle for tomorrow. Light comes from sources you can name — a desk lamp, a CRT, a marquee glow, a planetarium projector, a starfield — never from ambient gradient slop.

4. **Trinkets are the pavilion.** The blogroll, status feed, web buttons, bookshelf, and random-post list are exhibits in the wizard's pavilion. They are how the site does the indie-web job of pointing outward; every link is evidence that other people are also dreaming. Where they live and how they are named is open to redesign; that they are load-bearing is not.

5. **Legibility is sacred.** Atmosphere never wins over readability. Body type stays Atkinson Hyperlegible Next. Contrast meets WCAG 2.2 AA across all text, including wizardly ornaments and metadata. If a mystical or atompunk flourish dims the text below the floor, the flourish goes — not the floor.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- **Target: WCAG 2.2 AA** across all body, link, and metadata text on the dark night-shift surface. Verify every accent color against the surface lightness; nudge lightness rather than chroma when contrast falls short.
- **Body typography stays Atkinson Hyperlegible Next** (and Atkinson Hyperlegible Mono for code). Any decorative display face (mid-century geometric sans, ornamental italic, marquee letterform) carries the wizardly tone in headings and small labels; the body face must not.
- **Focus states are obvious.** Every interactive trinket (web buttons, blogroll links, status feed entries, tag pills, pagination, book covers) shows a visible focus ring distinct from hover.
- **Outbound links are honest.** Link affordances make link-versus-not-link unambiguous; no decorative underlines on non-links. The night-shift palette is committed enough that link styling must remain unmistakable on any surface where copy lives.
- **Reduced motion is respected.** Any planetarium-style rotation, slide-tray reveal, or marquee-glow flicker is gated behind `prefers-reduced-motion: no-preference`. The night shift is calm by default.
- **Color is not a sole carrier of meaning.** Tag categories, response indicators, and any accent-coded state also carry shape, icon, or labeled text.
